Why Marine Plastics?

This collection gives the concept of recycled and upcycled plastics a new perspective by utilizing the plastic trash and litter in our waterways, instead of plastics found only on land and in landfills.

Plastics and other marine debris are becoming a larger problem across the globe and creating garbage patches in both the Pacific and Atlantic oceans, as well as smaller bodies of water, such as the Mediterranean and North Seas.

Textiles with a purpose

As a part of our sustainability commitment, Valley Forge is dedicated to living and working together harmoniously with our environment. We have made the preservation of our plane and its resources a priority since 2002 by including recycled products in our standard product lines.

The 79 unique textiles within our first volume of Upcycled Marine Upholstery are woven with yarns that are made with plastics reclaimed from our oceands, coastal areas, and other waterways.

This includes plastics that are ocean-bound, meaning they have not made it to the ocean but are at high risk to end up there. The plastics are pulled from waterways through SEAQUAL INITIATIVE global clean-up partners, then sorted, washed, and twisted into the yarns that are then used to produce these hospitality-grade fabrics.

Designed for Hospitality

SEAQUAL® MARINE PLASTIC textiles weave sustainability, performance features, and comfort into one upholstery collection. The nine marine-inspired patterns come in an array of colors and textures that are as vibrant and diverse as the world’s oceans. The combination of neutral tones and vibrant hues create a fluid statement within hospitality spaces along with a pleasant mix of soft chenille and textured boucle styles, providing enough variety to satisfy all tiers of interior design.

Each sustainable pattern exceeds 50,000 double rubs and is woven in the USA to produce a unique and extensive line of upholstery fabrics.
